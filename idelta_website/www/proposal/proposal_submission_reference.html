Reference checklist and gap-closure guide for the AIPMS and FEVA proposal packages.

Purpose

This file is a working reference, not part of the bid itself. It tracks what is already strong in each proposal, what still needs to be supplied before submission, and which items should be handled as appendices instead of inflating the main proposal narrative.

What Not to Add

  • Do not bulk up the main proposal with repeated marketing language.
  • Do not repeat the same technical point in multiple sections just to make the document longer.
  • Do not bury critical evidence inside long narrative blocks; use appendices and clearly-labelled tables.
  • Do not leave placeholders in any final submission copy.

Questions to Resolve Before Submission

These are the main decision questions that still affect pricing, staffing, schedule credibility, or proposal positioning. They should be resolved before the final PDFs are issued.

Question Why It Matters Affects
Are AIPMS and FEVA expected to run concurrently if both are awarded? Concurrent delivery changes staffing commitments, timeline credibility, and whether the same named resources can appear in both bids without raising delivery risk. Both proposals, team section, delivery plan, commercials
Will the project teams work primarily from the State, from Abuja, or in a mixed delivery model? Location assumptions affect travel, logistics, field support, hypercare, and how strong the “local presence” claim can be made. Both proposals, pricing, support plan
Do we already have a named in-State partner or local operational contact? If yes, that can strengthen implementation confidence. If no, the proposal should avoid overstating in-State operational coverage. Main narrative, references, support model
Are the same core personnel genuinely available for both projects over the same period? This affects whether one shared team can be named across both submissions or whether each bid needs more differentiated staffing. Team tables, CV appendix, delivery credibility
For FEVA, who is the named GIS specialist and do we have a CV ready? The RFP explicitly values GIS capability. Leaving that role weak or vague will hurt scoring. FEVA technical score, appendix pack
For AIPMS, who are the named HR specialist and DevOps / hosting lead? Those placeholders need to be closed before submission, especially because payroll and hosting are central to the AIPMS scope. AIPMS team section, appendix pack
Do we want to keep the aggressive accelerated timelines, or soften them to match the RFP maximum timetable? A faster timeline is attractive, but only if the team and assumptions support it. Otherwise it can create unnecessary delivery risk. Executive summary, timeline section, commercial risk
Are the NGN commercial estimates intended as real bid prices or just planning placeholders? If they are still internal estimates, they should be reviewed before submission so the bid does not lock us into underpriced delivery. Section E in both proposals
For FEVA, do we want to price optional devices as excluded, included, or separately available on request? The current draft leaves device supply out. That is fine if deliberate, but it should be an explicit commercial choice. FEVA commercial proposal
Do we have usable client references that specifically match government, GIS, mobile fieldwork, or ERP programme control? Generic references are weaker. Better-matched references can materially improve scoring and evaluator confidence. Both proposals, appendices
Do we have the legal and statutory documents ready in final form? These are often treated as basic compliance gates. A good proposal can still fail if the statutory pack is incomplete. Final submission pack
Should we state our working assumptions explicitly in each proposal? This protects us from overcommitting where delivery depends on IGSL access, sample data, approvals, shapefiles, or stakeholder responsiveness. Both proposals, risk management

Suggested Internal Clarification Prompts

  • “If both projects land, what exact team do we have available from Week 1 to Week 8?”
  • “Are we budgeting for travel from Abuja only, or sustained in-State presence?”
  • “Which roles are permanent staff and which are partner or contract resources?”
  • “Which figures in the current commercial sections are already approved and which are still placeholders in disguise?”
  • “Which three references are strongest for AIPMS, and which three are strongest for FEVA?”
